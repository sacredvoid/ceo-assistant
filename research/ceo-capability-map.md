# CEO Assistant - Capability Map

A reference document covering the 10 domains the CEO Assistant handles, with specific tasks, frameworks, formulas, and benchmarks for each.

---

## 1. Strategy & Vision

**Purpose:** Help the CEO articulate where the company is going, why it matters, and how to get there.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Vision Statements | Craft compelling 3-5 year vision narratives |
| BMC / Lean Canvas | Map business models on a single page |
| SWOT Analysis | Structured strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats |
| Strategic Planning | Annual and multi-year strategic plans |
| Core Values & Focus | Define company DNA and strategic filter (EOS) |
| Rocks (90-Day Priorities) | Set and track quarterly priorities for the leadership team |
| Scenario Planning | Model best-case, base-case, worst-case futures |

### Frameworks

| Framework | What It Does | Key Components |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **EOS V/TO** | Defines the company's vision on two pages | 8 Questions: Core Values, Core Focus, 10-Year Target, Marketing Strategy, 3-Year Picture, 1-Year Plan, Quarterly Rocks, Issues List |
| **Lean Canvas** | One-page startup business model | Problem, Solution, Key Metrics, Unique Value Prop, Unfair Advantage, Channels, Customer Segments, Cost Structure, Revenue Streams |
| **Business Model Canvas (BMC)** | Full business model mapping | Key Partners, Key Activities, Key Resources, Value Propositions, Customer Relationships, Channels, Customer Segments, Cost Structure, Revenue Streams |
| **Scaling Up (Verne Harnish)** | Growth framework across 4 pillars | People, Strategy, Execution, Cash |
| **4DX (4 Disciplines of Execution)** | Strategy execution system | (1) Focus on the Wildly Important, (2) Act on Lead Measures, (3) Keep a Compelling Scoreboard, (4) Create a Cadence of Accountability |

## 2. Financial Management

**Purpose:** Give the CEO a clear, honest picture of the company's financial health and trajectory.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Burn Rate Tracking | Monitor monthly cash consumption |
| Runway Projection | Calculate months of cash remaining under various scenarios |
| 3-5 Year Financial Models | Revenue, expense, and headcount projections |
| P&L / Cash Flow | Monthly and quarterly financial statements |
| Fundraising Scenarios | Model dilution and cash impact of different raise amounts |
| Unit Economics | Calculate per-customer profitability |
| Budget vs. Actuals | Track spending against plan with variance analysis |
| Scenario Modeling | Stress-test financials under optimistic, base, and pessimistic assumptions |

### Key Formulas

| Metric | Formula | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **Gross Burn** | Total monthly operating expenses | All cash going out the door |
| **Net Burn** | Revenue - Total Expenses | Negative = burning cash |
| **Runway** | Cash Balance / Net Burn Rate | Measured in months |
| **CAC** | Total Sales & Marketing Spend / New Customers Acquired | Fully loaded (salaries, tools, ads) |
| **LTV** | ARPU x Gross Margin % x (1 / Monthly Churn Rate) | Lifetime value of a customer |
| **LTV:CAC** | LTV / CAC | Target: 3:1 to 5:1 |
| **CAC Payback** | CAC / (ARPU x Gross Margin %) | Months to recoup acquisition cost; target <12 months |
| **Rule of 40** | Revenue Growth Rate % + Profit Margin % | Healthy SaaS companies score >= 40 |
| **MRR** | Sum of all monthly recurring revenue | Foundation metric for SaaS |
| **ARR** | MRR x 12 | Annualized recurring revenue |
| **NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** | (Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR | Target: >100%, best-in-class >120% |

## 3. Fundraising & Investor Relations

**Purpose:** Help the CEO raise capital efficiently and maintain strong investor relationships.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Pitch Decks | Build investor-ready presentations |
| Investor Updates | Monthly/quarterly emails to existing investors |
| Fundraising CRM | Track investor pipeline, meetings, follow-ups |
| SAFE / Convertible Modeling | Model dilution from SAFEs and convertible notes |
| Cap Table Management | Track ownership, option grants, and dilution |
| Board Decks | Prepare materials for board meetings |
| Due Diligence Checklist | Organize documents investors will request |
| Term Sheet Comparison | Side-by-side analysis of competing offers |

### Templates

| Template | Structure |
|----------|-----------|
| **Sequoia Pitch Deck** | Company Purpose, Problem, Solution, Why Now, Market Size, Product, Business Model, Team, Financials, Vision |
| **YC Application Format** | What do you make? Why this idea? Progress so far, Revenue, How you'll grow, What's new, Founder backgrounds |
| **SAFE Notes** | Valuation cap, discount rate, pro-rata rights, MFN clause; model post-money SAFE dilution |
| **Board Deck Sections** | Highlights/Lowlights, KPI Dashboard, Financial Update, Product Update, Go-to-Market, Team, Asks |

### Benchmarks

| Parameter | Typical Range |
|-----------|--------------|
| Option Pool (pre-round) | 10-20% |
| Dilution per Seed Round | 15-25% |
| Dilution per Series A | 20-30% |
| SAFE Valuation Cap (pre-seed) | $5-15M (varies by market) |
| Months between raise start and close | 3-6 months |

## 4. Product Management

**Purpose:** Help the CEO make sharp product decisions, validate PMF, and align the team on what to build next.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Product Roadmaps | Visual quarterly/annual roadmap tied to strategy |
| Feature Prioritization | Score and rank features by impact and effort |
| PRDs (Product Requirements Docs) | Detailed specs for engineering handoff |
| PMF Tracking | Monitor product-market fit signals over time |
| Customer Feedback Synthesis | Aggregate and theme feedback from all channels |
| Competitive Feature Matrices | Side-by-side feature comparison vs. competitors |
| Sprint Planning | Help structure two-week sprint goals |
| Release Notes | Draft customer-facing changelog |

### Frameworks

| Framework | How It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Sean Ellis PMF Test** | Survey: "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?" -- 40%+ answering "very disappointed" = PMF |
| **RICE Scoring** | (Reach x Impact x Confidence) / Effort -- quantifies feature priority |
| **MoSCoW** | Must Have, Should Have, Could Have, Won't Have -- for scoping releases |
| **Kano Model** | Categorize features as Basic, Performance, or Delighter -- guides investment |
| **Steve Blank Customer Development** | Discovery, Validation, Creation, Building -- iterative loop before scaling |
| **JTBD (Jobs to Be Done)** | "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]" -- uncovers real needs behind feature requests |

## 5. Marketing & Growth

**Purpose:** Help the CEO build a repeatable growth engine and tell the company's story effectively.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Marketing Strategy | Channel mix, positioning, messaging hierarchy |
| Content Calendar | Plan blog posts, social, newsletters, launches |
| Copywriting | Landing pages, email sequences, ad copy, taglines |
| Growth Experiments | Design and track A/B tests and growth loops |
| Channel CAC Analysis | Break down acquisition cost by channel |
| SEO Briefs | Keyword research and content outlines |
| Press Releases | Announce funding, launches, partnerships |
| Positioning Maps | Visual competitive positioning |
| Brand Voice Guide | Tone, style, vocabulary standards |

### Frameworks

| Framework | How It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| **AARRR (Pirate Metrics)** | Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral -- full funnel measurement |
| **Bullseye Framework** | Brainstorm all channels, test the most promising 3, double down on the one that works |
| **Growth Loops** | Design self-reinforcing cycles (user creates content, content attracts new user, new user creates content) |
| **April Dunford Positioning** | Competitive Alternatives, Unique Attributes, Value (for customer), Best-Fit Customers, Market Category -- structured positioning exercise |

## 6. Team & Hiring

**Purpose:** Help the CEO build the right team, compensate fairly, and create a high-performance culture.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Hiring Plans | Headcount projections aligned to budget and milestones |
| Job Descriptions | Role-specific JDs with clear outcomes |
| Interview Scorecards | Structured evaluation criteria per role |
| Compensation Benchmarking | Market-rate salary and equity analysis |
| Equity Allocation | Grant sizes by role and stage |
| Org Charts | Current and planned organizational structure |
| 1-on-1 Templates | Structured agenda for manager-report meetings |
| Performance Reviews | Quarterly or semi-annual review frameworks |
| Culture Docs | Mission, values, operating principles documentation |

### Equity Benchmarks

| Employee Group | Typical Equity Range | Notes |
|---------------|---------------------|-------|
| First 5 hires | 0.5 - 2.0% | Highest risk, biggest upside |
| Employees #6 - 20 | 0.1 - 0.5% | Still early, meaningful grants |
| Employees #21 - 50 | 0.05 - 0.2% | Scaling phase |
| Employees #50+ | 0.01 - 0.1% | Growth stage |

**Standard Vesting:** 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff (25% vests at 12 months, remainder monthly over 36 months).

### Frameworks

| Framework | How It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| **EOS People Analyzer (GWC)** | For each core value, rate +/- . Then: Gets it (understands the role), Wants it (genuinely motivated), Capacity to do it (skills and bandwidth). All three must be yes. |
| **Topgrading** | Chronological interview method covering every job held. Focuses on: what were you hired to do, what did you accomplish, what mistakes did you make, who was your boss and what would they say about you. |

## 7. Legal & Compliance

**Purpose:** Help the CEO avoid legal landmines and stay on top of corporate governance at each stage.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Legal Checklists by Stage | What legal work to prioritize at pre-seed, seed, Series A, etc. |
| NDAs | Generate mutual and one-way NDAs |
| Contractor Agreements | Independent contractor terms and IP assignment |
| Founders Agreement | Equity splits, vesting, roles, IP assignment, departure terms |
| Corporate Governance | Board structure, meeting minutes, resolutions |
| Regulatory Compliance | Industry-specific requirements (HIPAA, GDPR, SOX, etc.) |
| 83(b) Election Tracking | Ensure founders file within 30 days of restricted stock grant |
| IP Portfolio | Track patents, trademarks, trade secrets, copyrights |
| Privacy Policies | GDPR/CCPA-compliant privacy and terms of service |

### Typical Legal Costs by Stage

| Item | Estimated Cost | When |
|------|---------------|------|
| Incorporation (e.g., Stripe Atlas) | $500 - $2,000 | Day 1 |
| Founders Agreement | $2,000 - $5,000 | Day 1 |
| Option Plan (409A + plan docs) | $5,000 - $15,000 | Before first hire |
| Seed Round Legal (SAFE) | $0 - $2,000 | Fundraising |
| Series A Legal (priced round) | $20,000 - $50,000 | Series A |
| SOC 2 Type I | $20,000 - $50,000 | When enterprise customers require it |
| Trademark Registration | $1,000 - $3,000 | After name/brand is finalized |
| Patent Filing (provisional) | $5,000 - $15,000 | When defensible IP exists |

## 8. Operations (OKRs, Meetings, Reporting)

**Purpose:** Help the CEO run the company with a consistent operating rhythm and clear accountability.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| OKRs | Set and track quarterly Objectives and Key Results |
| L10 Meetings (EOS) | Run structured weekly leadership meetings |
| Scorecards | Weekly metrics dashboards per department |
| All-Hands Decks | Company-wide meeting presentations |
| Issues List (IDS) | Identify, Discuss, Solve framework for blockers |
| Weekly CEO Digest | Summarized report of key metrics, wins, risks |
| Action Item Tracking | Assign, track, and close to-dos from meetings |
| QBR Prep | Quarterly business review materials |
| SOPs | Standard operating procedures for repeatable processes |
| Decision Logs | Record key decisions with context and rationale |

### Frameworks

| Framework | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **OKRs** | Each Objective gets 3-5 Key Results. Target 60-70% achievement (stretch goals). Graded 0.0-1.0. Cascaded from company to team to individual. |
| **EOS L10 Meeting** | 90-minute weekly agenda: Segue (5 min), Scorecard (5 min), Rock Review (5 min), Customer/Employee Headlines (5 min), To-Do List (5 min), IDS (60 min), Conclude (5 min) |
| **Rocks** | 3-7 Rocks per person per quarter. Binary: done or not done. Leadership team targets 80%+ completion rate. |
| **IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve)** | State the real issue in one sentence, open discussion with no tangents, land on a solution with a clear owner and due date. |

## 9. Market Research & Competitive Intelligence

**Purpose:** Help the CEO understand the landscape, size the opportunity, and spot threats early.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Competitive Landscape | Map all direct and indirect competitors |
| Feature Matrices | Side-by-side product comparison tables |
| TAM / SAM / SOM | Size the total, serviceable, and obtainable market |
| Industry Trends | Track macro trends affecting the business |
| PEST / PESTLE Analysis | Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental factors |
| Porter's Five Forces | Assess industry competitive intensity |
| Win/Loss Analysis | Understand why deals were won or lost |
| Competitor Pricing | Track and compare pricing models |
| Patent Landscape | Monitor competitor IP filings |

### Frameworks

| Framework | How It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| **TAM (Top-Down)** | Start with industry reports and total market size, narrow by geography and segment |
| **TAM (Bottom-Up)** | Number of potential customers x Average revenue per customer |
| **TAM (Value Theory)** | Estimate the value your product creates and what share you can capture |
| **Porter's Five Forces** | (1) Threat of New Entrants, (2) Bargaining Power of Suppliers, (3) Bargaining Power of Buyers, (4) Threat of Substitutes, (5) Industry Rivalry |
| **Blue Ocean - Four Actions** | Which factors to Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, or Create to break out of competitive red oceans |

### TAM/SAM/SOM Definitions

| Level | Definition | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | Total demand for the product category globally | All project management software spend worldwide |
| **SAM** (Serviceable Addressable Market) | Segment of TAM you can reach with your business model | PM software for tech startups in North America |
| **SOM** (Serviceable Obtainable Market) | Realistic share you can capture in 2-3 years | PM software for YC-backed startups using Slack |

## 10. Customer Development

**Purpose:** Help the CEO deeply understand customers, validate assumptions, and build feedback loops into the company.

### Tasks

| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Interview Scripts | Structured question sets for discovery and validation interviews |
| Transcript Synthesis | Extract themes, quotes, and patterns from interviews |
| ICP / Personas | Define ideal customer profiles with demographics, pain points, and goals |
| Customer Journey Maps | Visualize the end-to-end customer experience |
| NPS Surveys | Design and distribute Net Promoter Score surveys |
| Churn Analysis | Investigate why customers leave and identify at-risk accounts |
| Health Scoring | Build composite scores predicting retention/churn |
| Research Studies | Design and run structured customer research |
| VoC (Voice of Customer) Programs | Ongoing systems for capturing and routing customer feedback |

### Frameworks

| Framework | How It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Steve Blank Customer Development** | 4 phases: Customer Discovery (find problem), Customer Validation (find repeatable sales), Customer Creation (scale demand), Company Building (scale org) |
| **Sean Ellis PMF Survey** | Core question: "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?" Measure % "very disappointed." 40%+ = product-market fit. |
| **JTBD Interviews** | Focus on the "switching moment": what was the old way, what triggered the search, what anxiety existed, what outcome was desired. Timeline mapping from first thought to purchase. |
| **Customer Health Score** | Composite of: product usage frequency, feature breadth, support tickets, NPS response, contract value trend, engagement with CSM. Weight each factor and assign Red/Yellow/Green. |

### Benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Minimum interviews for patterns | 30 - 50 | Patterns start emerging at 10-15, but 30+ gives confidence |
| NPS Score (Excellent) | 50+ | World-class companies score 50-80 |
| NPS Score (Good) | 30 - 49 | Solid but room to improve |
| NPS Score (Needs Work) | < 30 | Signals product or service issues |
| Customer interview cadence | 5 - 10 per week | During active discovery phases |
| Churn investigation interviews | Within 48 hours of cancellation | Catch customers while memory is fresh |

## Cross-Cutting: CEO Dashboard

A single-view dashboard consolidating the metrics that matter most. The CEO should be able to glance at this and know the health of the business.

### Key Metrics at a Glance

| Metric | Formula / Source | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|--------|----------|
| **MRR** | Sum of monthly recurring revenue | Growing month-over-month | Flat or declining for 2+ months |
| **ARR** | MRR x 12 | Tracking to plan | >20% below annual target |
| **NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** | (Start MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Start MRR | > 120% | < 100% (shrinking revenue base) |
| **LTV:CAC** | LTV / CAC | 3:1 to 5:1 | < 1:1 (losing money per customer) |
| **CAC Payback** | CAC / (ARPU x Gross Margin %) | < 12 months | > 18 months |
| **Growth Rate** | (Current MRR - Prior MRR) / Prior MRR | > 100% YoY (early stage) | Decelerating without plan |
| **Monthly Churn** | Lost customers / Starting customers | < 5% monthly | > 7% monthly |
| **Gross Burn** | Total monthly expenses | Within budget | > 120% of plan |
| **Runway** | Cash / Net Burn | > 18 months | < 6 months |
| **PMF Score** | % "very disappointed" in Sean Ellis survey | > 40% | < 25% |
| **NPS** | Promoters% - Detractors% | > 50 | < 20 |
| **eNPS (Employee NPS)** | Employee Promoters% - Employee Detractors% | > 30 | < 0 |
| **Rule of 40** | Revenue Growth % + Profit Margin % | >= 40 | < 20 |
| **Burn Multiple** | Net Burn / Net New ARR | < 2x | > 3x |

### Dashboard Zones

| Zone | Criteria | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Green** | All core metrics at or above target | Stay the course, invest in growth |
| **Yellow** | 1-2 metrics in red flag territory | Investigate root cause, adjust within 2 weeks |
| **Red** | 3+ metrics in red flag territory or runway < 6 months | Emergency leadership review, cut burn or raise immediately |
