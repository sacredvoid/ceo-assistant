# Customer Interview Script

Generate a Mom Test-style customer discovery interview script.

## Input
Ask: "What problem are you trying to validate? Who will you interview?"

## Process
Load framework from @ceo-assistant/docs/business-templates.md (Section 5: Validation Interview Script)

## Generated Script

### Opening (2 min)
- Warm-up about their role/day
- "Tell me about your work at [company]"

### Problem Discovery (10 min)
- "Walk me through the last time you [experienced problem area]?"
- "What's the most frustrating part of [process]?"
- "How are you currently solving this?"
- "What don't you love about your current approach?"

### Behavior & Impact (10 min)
- "How often does this come up? Daily? Weekly?"
- "What does this problem cost you - in time, money, or stress?"
- "What have you tried to fix it? What happened?"
- "When was the last time this really frustrated you?"

### Solution Exploration (5 min - LAST, not first)
- "If this problem just disappeared, what would that mean for you?"
- "How much would solving this be worth per month?"
- "Would you try an early version if I built something?"

### Close (3 min)
- "Who else has this problem? Can you introduce me?"
- "Can I follow up in 2 weeks with a prototype?"

## Rules (Non-Negotiable)
- NEVER pitch first, discover first
- NEVER ask "Would you use X?" (hypothetical, unreliable)
- NEVER ask leading questions ("Don't you think...?")
- Listen 80%, talk 20%
- Note BEHAVIORS and SPENDING, not opinions
- End every interview with a referral ask (3 names)

## Analysis (After 10-15 interviews)
Strong signals:
- 70%+ independently describe the same pain
- People spending money/time on workarounds
- Emotional language about the problem
- Specific recent examples (not vague generalities)
- "When can I buy this?"

Weak signals:
- "That's a great idea!" (compliment, not commitment)
- "I would definitely use that" (hypothetical)
- Vague, non-specific pain descriptions
- No current workarounds (pain isn't severe enough)

Target: 30-50 interviews. Patterns emerge at 10-15.
