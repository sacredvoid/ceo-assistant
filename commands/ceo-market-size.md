# Market Sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)

Calculate market size using both top-down and bottom-up methods with live research.

## Input
Ask: "What product/service are you building, and who is your target customer?"

## Step 1: Top-Down Research
Use WebSearch to find industry data:
- "[industry] market size 2025 2026"
- "[industry] CAGR forecast"
- Look for Statista, Gartner, IDC, Grand View Research reports
Extract: total market value, growth rate, geographic breakdown

## Step 2: Bottom-Up Calculation
Ask for (or estimate):
- Number of potential customers in target segment
- Average annual spend per customer (ACV)
- TAM = Customers x ACV

## Step 3: SAM (Serviceable Available Market)
Filter TAM by:
- Geographic reach
- Customer segment you can actually serve
- Product limitations
- SAM = TAM x reachable %

## Step 4: SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
- Year 1-2: 0.1-1% of SAM (realistic for most startups)
- Year 3-5: 1-5% of SAM (with traction)
- Compare to competitor market shares found via research

## Step 5: Validation
- Top-down and bottom-up align within 15%? Strong confidence.
- Diverge >15%? Flag assumptions to revisit.
- TAM > $1B for VC-backed? > $50M for bootstrapped?
