# Set OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)

Generate quarterly OKRs for the company and teams.

## Input
Ask: "What quarter is this for? What are your top 1-3 priorities?"

## Process

### Step 1: Draft Objectives
For each priority, create an Objective:
- Qualitative, inspiring, 1 sentence
- Starts with a verb
- Achievable within the quarter
- Example: "Establish product-market fit with our core segment"

### Step 2: Draft Key Results
For each Objective, draft 3-5 Key Results:
- Must be quantitative and measurable
- Must have a specific number/percentage
- Format: "[Verb] [metric] from [current] to [target]"
- Should be stretch goals (expect 60-70% achievement)

Examples:
- "Increase MRR from $10K to $30K"
- "Achieve 40%+ 'very disappointed' score on Sean Ellis PMF survey"
- "Reduce monthly churn from 8% to 4%"
- "Ship 3 features from the top-10 customer request list"

### Step 3: Validate
- Are KRs measurable without ambiguity?
- Can you check progress weekly?
- Are they stretch but not impossible?
- Do they connect to company-level goals?
- Is there a mix of leading and lagging indicators?

## Output

### Q[X] 20XX OKRs - [Company Name]

**Objective 1:** [Statement]
- KR1: [Metric from X to Y] - Owner: [name]
- KR2: [Metric from X to Y] - Owner: [name]
- KR3: [Metric from X to Y] - Owner: [name]

**Objective 2:** [Statement]
...

### Scoring Guide (end of quarter)
- 0.0-0.3: Failed to make meaningful progress
- 0.4-0.6: Made progress but fell significantly short
- 0.7: Sweet spot for stretch goals
- 0.8-1.0: Delivered fully (if consistently hitting 1.0, goals aren't stretchy enough)

## EOS Integration
Offer: "Want me to also create 90-day Rocks (EOS format) and a weekly scorecard to track these?"

### Rocks Format
Each Rock: [Owner] - [Specific deliverable] - Due [date]
- 3-7 per person
- SMART formatted
- 80%+ completion target
- Review weekly in L10 meeting
