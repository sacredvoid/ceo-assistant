# Startup Glossary

Quick-lookup reference for startup terminology, organized by category and alphabetized within each group. Benchmarks and thresholds included where applicable.

---

## Market Sizing

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Addressable Market Growth** | Rate at which a target market is expanding year-over-year, measured by CAGR. | >15% CAGR = expanding market |
| **Beachhead Market** | A small, well-defined market segment you dominate first before expanding outward. | Pick the narrowest niche you can own completely |
| **Blue Ocean Strategy** | Creating uncontested market space rather than competing in crowded existing markets. | - |
| **Market Fragmentation** | A market with many small players and no dominant leader, signaling consolidation opportunity. | No single player >10-15% share = fragmented |
| **Market Opportunity** | The total revenue potential available if a product captures its intended audience. | - |
| **Market Saturation** | The point at which a market has absorbed as much of a product as it can, limiting new growth. | Slowing growth + pricing pressure = saturation signals |
| **Market Timing** | Whether market conditions, technology readiness, and customer awareness align for a product launch. | - |
| **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)** | The portion of TAM your product can realistically target given your business model and geography. | Typically 10-30% of TAM |
| **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)** | The slice of SAM you can realistically capture in the near term (1-3 years). | 1-5% of SAM for early-stage startups |
| **TAM (Total Addressable Market)** | The total market demand for a product or service if you had 100% market share and zero constraints. | $1B+ TAM preferred by VCs |

## Competitive Analysis

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Competitive Advantage** | A condition or capability that allows a company to outperform its rivals consistently. | - |
| **Competitor Analysis** | Systematic evaluation of competing companies' strengths, weaknesses, strategies, and market positioning. | - |
| **Differentiation** | The specific attributes that make your product distinct and preferable to alternatives. | - |
| **Fast-Follower Strategy** | Entering a market after a pioneer, learning from their mistakes, and executing better or cheaper. | - |
| **First-Mover Advantage** | The competitive edge gained by being the first to enter a market, building brand recognition and switching costs. | Strongest with network effects or high switching costs |
| **Moat** | A durable competitive advantage that protects a business from competitors (network effects, switching costs, brand, cost advantages, IP). | Wider moat = harder to replicate = more defensible |
| **UVP (Unique Value Proposition)** | A clear statement of the specific benefit your product delivers that no competitor matches. | Should be expressible in one sentence |

## Financial Metrics

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** | The annualized value of recurring subscription revenue, normalized to a one-year period. | $1M ARR = common milestone for Series A readiness |
| **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)** | Total revenue divided by number of users over a given period. | Varies by vertical; track trend over time |
| **Break-Even Point** | The moment when total revenue equals total costs, and the business stops losing money. | - |
| **Burn Rate** | The rate at which a company spends cash reserves before generating positive cash flow. | Monthly burn = total operating expenses - revenue |
| **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** | The total cost of acquiring a new customer, including marketing, sales, and onboarding. | SaaS avg: $200-500 (SMB), $5K-50K+ (enterprise) |
| **Cash Flow** | The net amount of cash moving in and out of the business over a given period. | Positive cash flow = self-sustaining |
| **Churn Rate** | The percentage of customers or revenue lost over a specific time period. | <5% annual churn (enterprise SaaS), <7% monthly (consumer) |
| **Gross Margin** | Revenue minus cost of goods sold, expressed as a percentage of revenue. | >70% for SaaS, >50% for marketplaces |
| **LTV (Lifetime Value)** | The total revenue a business expects from a single customer over their entire relationship. | - |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | The ratio of customer lifetime value to acquisition cost, measuring return on acquisition spend. | 3:1+ = healthy; <1:1 = losing money per customer |
| **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)** | The predictable, normalized monthly revenue from active subscriptions. | Track MoM growth rate; 15-20% MoM = strong early stage |
| **NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** | Revenue retained from existing customers after churn, contraction, and expansion, expressed as a percentage. | >100% = growing without new customers; >120% = elite |
| **Payback Period** | The time it takes to recover the cost of acquiring a customer through their revenue. | <12 months = healthy SaaS; <6 months = excellent |
| **Runway** | The number of months a company can operate before running out of cash at the current burn rate. | 12-18 months minimum; fundraise at 6 months remaining |
| **Unit Economics** | The direct revenues and costs associated with a single unit of your business model (per customer, per transaction). | Must be positive to scale sustainably |

## Product

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Core Feature Set** | The minimum set of features that define the product's identity and deliver its primary value. | - |
| **Customer Journey Map** | A visual representation of every interaction a customer has with your product from awareness through retention. | - |
| **Customer Pain Point** | A specific, recurring problem that a target customer experiences and would pay to solve. | Stronger pain = higher willingness to pay |
| **Feature Creep** | The gradual, uncontrolled addition of features beyond the original scope, diluting focus and increasing complexity. | - |
| **JTBD (Jobs To Be Done)** | A framework that defines products by the "job" a customer hires them to accomplish, not by features. | - |
| **Killer Feature** | A single standout capability so compelling it drives adoption on its own. | - |
| **MVP (Minimum Viable Product)** | The simplest version of a product that delivers enough value to attract early adopters and generate learning. | Ship in weeks, not months |
| **Problem-Solution Fit** | Validation that a proposed solution actually addresses a real customer problem worth solving. | Precedes product-market fit |
| **Product Iteration** | The cycle of releasing, measuring, learning, and improving a product based on real user data. | - |
| **Product-Market Fit** | The stage where a product satisfies strong market demand, evidenced by organic growth and retention. | Sean Ellis test: 40%+ "very disappointed" threshold |
| **Technical Debt** | The accumulated cost of shortcuts and suboptimal code that must eventually be addressed to maintain velocity. | Allocate 10-20% of engineering time to pay it down |
| **User Persona** | A semi-fictional profile representing a key segment of your target users, based on research and data. | - |

## Business Models

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Advertising Model** | Revenue generated by showing ads to users, monetizing attention and data rather than charging for the product. | Requires massive scale; CPM typically $2-10 |
| **B2B (Business-to-Business)** | Selling products or services to other businesses rather than individual consumers. | Higher ACV, longer sales cycles, lower churn |
| **B2C (Business-to-Consumer)** | Selling directly to individual end consumers. | Lower ACV, shorter cycles, higher volume needed |
| **D2C (Direct-to-Consumer)** | Selling directly to consumers without intermediaries like retailers or distributors. | Higher margins but must own customer acquisition |
| **Freemium** | Offering a free base product with paid premium features or tiers to drive conversion. | 2-5% free-to-paid conversion = typical; >5% = strong |
| **Licensing Model** | Revenue from granting rights to use proprietary technology, IP, or content for a fee. | - |
| **Marketplace** | A platform connecting buyers and sellers, earning revenue through transaction fees or commissions. | Take rate typically 10-30% |
| **On-Demand Model** | Providing goods or services immediately when the customer requests them, often via a digital platform. | - |
| **Platform Business Model** | A business that creates value by facilitating exchanges between two or more interdependent groups. | Defensibility grows with network effects |
| **SaaS (Software as a Service)** | Cloud-delivered software sold on a recurring subscription basis. | Gross margins >75%, NRR >100% |
| **Subscription** | A recurring payment model where customers pay periodically for ongoing access to a product or service. | Monthly or annual; annual = better retention + cash flow |
| **Transaction Model** | Revenue earned by taking a cut of each transaction processed through the platform. | - |

## Funding & Investment

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Angel Investor** | An individual who provides early-stage capital in exchange for equity, often with mentorship. | Typical check: $25K-250K |
| **Bootstrapping** | Building a company using personal savings and revenue, without external funding. | Full control retained; slower growth trajectory |
| **Cap Table** | A spreadsheet or ledger showing who owns what percentage of the company across all share classes. | Keep clean; messy cap tables scare investors |
| **Equity Dilution** | The reduction in existing shareholders' ownership percentage when new shares are issued. | 15-25% dilution per funding round is typical |
| **Exit Strategy** | The planned method for founders and investors to realize returns (acquisition, IPO, secondary sale). | - |
| **Pitch Deck** | A concise presentation (10-15 slides) used to communicate your business to potential investors. | Problem, solution, market, traction, team, ask |
| **Pre-Money Valuation** | The assessed value of a company before it receives new investment. | - |
| **Seed Round** | The first significant round of institutional or angel funding to prove a concept and reach early milestones. | Typical range: $500K-3M |
| **Series A/B/C** | Successive funding rounds of increasing size. A = scale what works ($5-15M); B = expand ($15-50M); C = dominate ($50M+). | Each round requires demonstrably stronger metrics |
| **Term Sheet** | A non-binding document outlining the key terms and conditions of a proposed investment. | - |
| **Unicorn** | A privately held startup valued at $1 billion or more. | $1B+ valuation |
| **Venture Capital** | Professional investment firms that fund high-growth startups in exchange for equity. | Expect 10x+ return; board seat typical |

## Growth & Marketing

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **A/B Testing** | Running two variants of a page, feature, or message simultaneously to measure which performs better. | Statistical significance required (p < 0.05) |
| **Content Marketing** | Attracting and retaining customers by creating valuable, relevant content rather than direct advertising. | - |
| **Conversion Rate** | The percentage of users who take a desired action (sign up, purchase, upgrade). | SaaS trial-to-paid: 15-25% = good |
| **Funnel Optimization** | Systematically improving each stage of the customer journey to reduce drop-off and increase conversions. | - |
| **Growth Hacking** | Rapid experimentation across marketing, product, and engineering to find scalable growth levers. | - |
| **GTM Strategy (Go-to-Market)** | The plan for launching a product to market, covering channels, pricing, positioning, and sales motion. | - |
| **PLG (Product-Led Growth)** | A strategy where the product itself drives acquisition, expansion, and retention with minimal sales involvement. | Self-serve sign-up + in-product upsells |
| **Referral Program** | A structured system rewarding existing users for bringing in new customers. | - |
| **Sales-Led Growth** | A strategy where dedicated sales teams drive customer acquisition through outbound outreach and demos. | Common in enterprise B2B |
| **Viral Coefficient** | The number of new users each existing user generates through sharing or invitations. | >1.0 = exponential growth; 0.5-1.0 = good amplifier |

## Operations & Frameworks

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **AARRR (Pirate Metrics)** | A five-stage funnel framework: Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral. | Track conversion rates between each stage |
| **EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System)** | A holistic business management system with tools for vision, traction, and organizational health. | - |
| **IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve)** | An EOS meeting technique for resolving issues by first clearly identifying the root cause before discussing solutions. | - |
| **Kano Model** | A framework that classifies product features by how they affect customer satisfaction (must-have, performance, delighter). | - |
| **L10 Meeting** | A weekly 90-minute leadership meeting structured by EOS, focused on scorecard review, rocks, and IDS. | 90 minutes, same day/time each week |
| **MoSCoW** | A prioritization framework: Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have (this time). | - |
| **NPS (Net Promoter Score)** | A loyalty metric based on the question "How likely are you to recommend us?" scored from -100 to 100. | >50 = excellent; >70 = world-class |
| **OKR (Objectives and Key Results)** | A goal-setting framework pairing qualitative objectives with measurable key results. | 3-5 objectives per quarter, 2-4 KRs each |
| **RICE Scoring** | A prioritization framework scoring features by Reach, Impact, Confidence, and Effort. | Score = (R x I x C) / E |
| **Rocks (90-day priorities)** | EOS term for the 3-7 most important priorities that must be completed in the current quarter. | 3-7 per person/team per quarter |
| **Rule of 40** | A SaaS health metric where growth rate + profit margin should exceed 40%. | >40% = healthy; companies above often command premium valuations |
| **Scorecard** | An EOS tool tracking 5-15 weekly activity-based metrics that predict future results. | - |
| **Sean Ellis Test** | A product-market fit survey asking "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?" | 40%+ answering "very disappointed" = PMF achieved |
| **V/TO (Vision/Traction Organizer)** | An EOS document capturing the company's vision, target market, 10-year goal, 3-year picture, and 1-year plan. | - |

## Legal

| Term | Definition | Benchmark |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **83(b) Election** | An IRS filing allowing founders to pay tax on equity at grant date (low value) rather than at vesting (higher value). | Must file within 30 days of grant; missing the deadline is irreversible |
| **409A Valuation** | An independent appraisal of a private company's common stock fair market value, required for issuing stock options. | Must update annually or after material events |
| **Convertible Note** | A short-term debt instrument that converts into equity at a future funding round, typically with a discount and/or cap. | Common discount: 15-25%; cap negotiated per deal |
| **GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)** | EU regulation governing how companies collect, store, and process personal data of EU residents. | Non-compliance fines up to 4% of global annual revenue |
| **HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)** | US regulation requiring safeguards for protected health information (PHI) in healthcare-related products. | Required for any product handling patient data |
| **Option Pool** | A block of shares reserved for future employee stock option grants, typically created before a funding round. | 10-20% of fully diluted shares is standard |
| **SAFE Note (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)** | A Y Combinator-created investment instrument that converts to equity at a future priced round, simpler than a convertible note. | No interest, no maturity date; valuation cap is the key term |
| **SOC 2 (Service Organization Control 2)** | An auditing standard verifying that a company handles customer data securely across five trust principles. | Often required by enterprise B2B buyers |
| **Vesting** | A schedule determining when equity actually becomes owned by the recipient. | Standard: 4-year schedule with 1-year cliff (25% at year 1, then monthly) |
