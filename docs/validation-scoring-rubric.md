# Validation Scoring Rubric

Weighted rubric for scoring startup idea viability. Total score: 0-100.

## Scoring Instructions
For each criterion, score 1-10 using the guidelines below. Multiply by weight. Sum all weighted scores for final score.

## The 15 Criteria

### 1. Market Timing (Weight: 12%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Perfect timing: enabling technology just matured, regulation just changed, behavior shift underway. Clear "why now" that didn't exist 2 years ago. |
| 7-8 | Good timing: market is growing, tailwinds present, but not uniquely urgent. |
| 5-6 | Neutral: market exists but no specific timing advantage. |
| 3-4 | Slightly early: technology or market not quite ready, 1-2 year wait. |
| 1-2 | Too early or too late: market hasn't formed, or already mature/declining. |

### 2. Market Size - TAM (Weight: 10%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | TAM > $10B, bottom-up validated, growing >20% CAGR |
| 7-8 | TAM $1-10B, credible data sources, growing >10% CAGR |
| 5-6 | TAM $100M-1B, moderate growth |
| 3-4 | TAM $10-100M, slow growth or stagnant |
| 1-2 | TAM < $10M or shrinking market |

### 3. Market Growth Rate (Weight: 8%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | >25% CAGR, expanding rapidly |
| 7-8 | 15-25% CAGR, strong growth |
| 5-6 | 5-15% CAGR, moderate |
| 3-4 | 0-5% CAGR, flat |
| 1-2 | Negative growth, declining |

### 4. Problem Severity (Weight: 8%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Hair-on-fire problem. People actively searching for solutions, spending money on workarounds, experiencing daily pain. |
| 7-8 | Significant pain. People complain about it regularly, have tried multiple solutions. |
| 5-6 | Moderate pain. Problem exists but people cope with it. Nice-to-have territory. |
| 3-4 | Mild inconvenience. People notice it occasionally but don't prioritize solving it. |
| 1-2 | Not a real problem. Solution looking for a problem. |

### 5. Willingness to Pay (Weight: 8%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | People already paying for inferior alternatives. Budget exists. Multiple signals of payment intent. |
| 7-8 | People would clearly pay. Comparable products exist at similar price points. |
| 5-6 | Some willingness to pay but price sensitivity. Free alternatives dominate. |
| 3-4 | Unclear monetization. Users expect free. Weak payment signals. |
| 1-2 | No evidence anyone would pay. Market expects free forever. |

### 6. Competition Intensity (Weight: 7%)
(Note: LOWER competition = HIGHER score, but ZERO competition = LOW score because it suggests no market)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | 3-10 competitors exist (validating demand) but all have clear weaknesses you can exploit. No dominant winner. |
| 7-8 | Competition exists, market fragmented, room for differentiation. |
| 5-6 | Moderate competition. 1-2 well-funded players but gaps remain. |
| 3-4 | Intense competition. Well-funded incumbents with strong products. Or: zero competitors (likely no market). |
| 1-2 | Winner-take-all market with dominant incumbent (>60% share). Or: truly zero demand. |

### 7. Differentiation Strength (Weight: 7%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Clear, defensible differentiation. 10x better on a dimension that matters. Unique data, technology, or approach. |
| 7-8 | Strong differentiation on 2-3 important dimensions. |
| 5-6 | Some differentiation but could be copied within 6-12 months. |
| 3-4 | Weak differentiation. Mostly feature parity with slight improvements. |
| 1-2 | No meaningful differentiation. "Me too" product. |

### 8. Business Model Clarity (Weight: 6%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Proven business model in this market. Clear revenue streams, pricing validated, unit economics work on paper. |
| 7-8 | Business model defined and plausible. Similar models work for competitors. |
| 5-6 | Business model exists but untested. Some uncertainty about pricing or monetization. |
| 3-4 | Vague monetization. "We'll figure it out later." |
| 1-2 | No business model. Pure growth play with no path to revenue. |

### 9. Unit Economics Feasibility (Weight: 6%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | LTV:CAC clearly >5:1 based on comparable businesses. Gross margins >70%. |
| 7-8 | LTV:CAC likely 3-5:1. Healthy margins for the industry. |
| 5-6 | LTV:CAC around 2-3:1. Workable but tight. |
| 3-4 | LTV:CAC likely <2:1. Margins thin. May need scale to work. |
| 1-2 | Unit economics don't work. CAC likely exceeds LTV. |

### 10. Founder-Market Fit (Weight: 6%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Deep domain expertise. Previously worked in this industry. Has unfair access to customers, data, or distribution. |
| 7-8 | Relevant experience. Strong skills for this market. Some industry connections. |
| 5-6 | General skills applicable. Smart and capable but no domain advantage. |
| 3-4 | Limited relevant experience. Would need to learn the industry from scratch. |
| 1-2 | No relevant skills or experience. Wrong founder for this problem. |

### 11. Technical Feasibility (Weight: 5%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Can build MVP in 2-4 weeks with existing technology. No technical risk. |
| 7-8 | Technically feasible. Standard tech stack. 1-3 month MVP. |
| 5-6 | Some technical challenges but solvable. 3-6 month MVP. |
| 3-4 | Significant technical risk. Requires R&D. 6-12 months to prototype. |
| 1-2 | Requires breakthrough technology that doesn't exist yet. |

### 12. Regulatory Risk (Weight: 5%)
(LOWER risk = HIGHER score)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | No regulatory requirements. Standard software/services. |
| 7-8 | Light regulation. Standard compliance (GDPR, basic privacy). |
| 5-6 | Moderate regulation. Industry-specific compliance needed (SOC 2, PCI). $50-100K cost. |
| 3-4 | Heavy regulation. Licenses required, long approval timelines (fintech, healthcare). $100K-500K+ cost. |
| 1-2 | Extreme regulatory barriers. FDA approval, banking licenses, multi-year timelines. |

### 13. Scalability (Weight: 4%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Pure software, near-zero marginal cost, global from day 1. |
| 7-8 | Highly scalable with some operational cost per customer. |
| 5-6 | Scalable but requires proportional headcount or infrastructure growth. |
| 3-4 | Difficult to scale. High-touch, services-heavy, geographic constraints. |
| 1-2 | Doesn't scale. One-to-one service model. |

### 14. Distribution Advantage (Weight: 4%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Built-in distribution: viral mechanics, network effects, existing audience, platform marketplace. |
| 7-8 | Strong distribution channels identified. Partnerships or communities available. |
| 5-6 | Standard distribution. Will need paid acquisition and content marketing. |
| 3-4 | Distribution is expensive and unclear. Long sales cycles. |
| 1-2 | No clear path to customers. Cold outbound to enterprise only. |

### 15. Defensibility / Moat (Weight: 4%)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Strong moat: proprietary data, network effects, patents, switching costs, brand. |
| 7-8 | Moderate moat: early-mover advantage, niche expertise, growing data asset. |
| 5-6 | Weak moat: could be replicated in 6-12 months but execution is the advantage. |
| 3-4 | Very weak moat. Anyone with money could build this. |
| 1-2 | No defensibility whatsoever. Commodity product. |

## Score Calculation
Final Score = Sum of (Each Score x Weight x 10)

Example: If criterion 1 (Market Timing, weight 12%) gets score 8:
Weighted contribution = 8 x 0.12 x 10 = 9.6 points

## Interpretation
| Score Range | Rating | Historical Success Rate | Recommendation |
|------------|--------|------------------------|----------------|
| 85-100 | Exceptional | 89% | Proceed with confidence. Start building MVP. |
| 75-84 | Strong | 75% | Address flagged weaknesses, then proceed. |
| 65-74 | Moderate | 52% | Validate further before committing resources. |
| 50-64 | Weak | 28% | Major pivots needed. Consider alternative ideas. |
| 0-49 | Very Weak | 8% | Do not pursue. Explore different ideas. |
